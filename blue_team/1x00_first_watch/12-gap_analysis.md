### Gap Distribution Summary

**Gaps by Risk Level:**
- **Critical (5):** GAP-001 (medical IoT), GAP-002 (SIEM), GAP-005 (physical audit), GAP-006 (IR plan), GAP-007 (BCP/DRP), GAP-009 (Windows XP)
- **High (6):** GAP-003 (encryption at rest), GAP-004 (Westside network), GAP-008 (endpoint controls), GAP-010 (encryption in transit), GAP-011 (Westside endpoint)

**Gaps by Asset Category (Most Exposed):**
- **Network Infrastructure (3 gaps):** GAP-001 (segmentation), GAP-002 (detection), GAP-004 (Westside equipment)
- **Clinical Systems (4 gaps):** GAP-001 (IoT), GAP-007 (BCP), GAP-009 (Windows XP), GAP-011 (Westside clinic)
- **Data Protection (3 gaps):** GAP-003 (encryption at rest), GAP-010 (encryption in transit)
- **Operational Resilience (2 gaps):** GAP-006 (incident response), GAP-007 (business continuity)

**Control Category Distribution:**
- **Missing Technical Preventive controls:** 7 gaps (network segmentation, encryption, endpoint controls, compensating controls for legacy systems)
- **Missing Technical Detective controls:** 4 gaps (SIEM, IDS, behavior monitoring, access audit trail)
- **Missing Administrative Corrective controls:** 2 gaps (incident response, business continuity)

**Implication:** MedDefense's gaps are concentrated in **detection and resilience.** The organization has preventive perimeter controls (firewall) but no ability to detect compromise once preventive controls fail, and no procedures to respond or recover when incidents occur. This creates a scenario where each incident (January ransomware, June crypto-miner, February portal breach) repeats the same failure: discover the breach after damage, respond ad-hoc without procedure, learn nothing systematically.
