### Data Risk Summary

**MedDefense's Most Significant Data Protection Weakness:**

Patient medical records (Restricted classification) in the EHR database are protected at rest only by OS-level access control and perimeter firewall — there is no encryption at rest, no database-level encryption, and no detection of unauthorized database queries. In transit, EHR data travels unencrypted across the flat 10.10.0.0/16 network accessible to 380+ workstations, medical devices, and servers, any of which could be compromised. In use, clinician workstations display sensitive patient data with no screen lock policy (Exercise 3 Observation 3: idle EHR session displaying patient record for 15+ minutes, unattended). These gaps combine to create a scenario where an attacker who compromises a single clinical workstation (via phishing malware, USB, or physical access) can exfiltrate the entire EHR database (~50,000 patient records) without detection. The January ransomware incident and June crypto-miner (both undetected for extended periods) demonstrate that this scenario is not theoretical — it is occurring. The protection gap is not in a single state (rest, transit, or use) but rather in the *absence of encryption across all states combined with absence of detection capability* — meaning compromise is possible and undetectable.
