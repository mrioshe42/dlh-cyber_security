## 17: The CISO Briefing

### MedDefense Security Briefing for Board of Directors

**Current Posture:** MedDefense Health Systems has strong firewalls and backups but no way to detect attackers already inside our network. We have experienced three security incidents in six months (system lockout in January, data breach in February, and unauthorized cryptocurrency mining in June) and cannot determine whether attackers still have access.

**Critical Finding:** Attackers can trick employees into opening malicious emails, gain access to the patient records system containing 50,000 records, change medication orders, or lock critical systems without being detected until patient harm occurs or systems stop working. A single attack could disrupt hospital operations for 7–10 days, cost $2–5M in recovery, lost revenue, and regulatory penalties.

**Three Priority Actions:**

1. **Deploy continuous security monitoring: $60K, 8 weeks** — Gives us real-time visibility so attacks are detected within hours instead of weeks.

2. **Develop an emergency cyber response plan: $10K, 1 month** — Establishes clear procedures so future attacks are contained within hours, greatly reducing downtime and data loss.

3. **Separate medical devices from the main network: $10K, 3 weeks** — Prevents attackers from reaching infusion pumps, patient monitors, and other clinical systems, directly protecting patient safety.

**Business Case:** These investments total **$80K** ($50K in Year 1 and $30K in Year 2). One major cyberattack costs $2–5M, notifying 50,000 affected patients exceeds $500K, and a patient harm event could create more than $10M in liability. This investment protects patients, reduces financial risk, and strengthens hospital operations.

**If We Take No Action:** Another major cyberattack is likely within 12 months. Without better monitoring or a response plan, hospital operations could be severely disrupted, patients transferred elsewhere, and recovery significantly delayed. Immediate action is essential now.

Every delay increases financial losses and threatens patient safety and hospital operations.
