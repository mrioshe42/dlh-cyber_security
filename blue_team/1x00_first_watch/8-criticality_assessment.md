### Top 5 Most Critical Assets

**1. EHR System (ehr-srv-01 + ehr-db-01)**
The EHR is the single point of truth for patient care. Every clinical decision at MedDefense — diagnosis, medication, surgery scheduling, lab orders, imaging interpretation — depends on data in the EHR. Compromise of this system (ransomware, unauthorized access, data corruption) directly threatens patient safety and organizational viability. A 24-hour EHR outage would force the hospital to closure-level response. Data breach of 50,000 patient records triggers regulatory notification, potential $100M+ in fines, litigation and reputational damage that could threaten the organization's financial survival.

**2. Infusion Pump Network (BD Alaris + Patient Monitors)**
Life-safety-critical infrastructure. Unlike the EHR (where workarounds exist — paper records), there is no workaround for disconnecting infusion pumps or vital signs monitors. Compromise of these devices (dosage modification, false vital signs) has no offset; the patient is harmed directly. A single pump exploited to deliver 10x the prescribed dose to a single patient could be fatal. The fact that 120 pumps are on the unmanaged network, on the same VLAN as workstations and servers, makes this the highest patient-safety risk in the organization.

**3. MRI Scanner (Siemens MAGNETOM Windows XP)**
Unpatched Windows XP in a patient care environment represents a vulnerability that cannot be remediated (OS upgrade voids certification). The combination of legacy OS + network connectivity + flat network makes this a guaranteed compromise waiting to happen. Once compromised, the attacker has persistence on a system that bridges medical device network and the flat network; pivot point to imaging archive, workstations, EHR.

**4. Network Core (Cisco switches + FortiGate firewall)**
The network is the circulatory system. Compromise of core switches or firewall enables an attacker to see, intercept or redirect all traffic in the organization. Flat network (no VLANs, no segmentation) amplifies this risk: a single compromised switch can segment the network, disable firewall rules, or redirect traffic to an attacker's system. The lack of network monitoring (G-001, Exercise 5) means such compromise would be undetected until clinical impact occurs.

**5. Billing System (billing-srv-01)**
Financial data is high-value to attackers (payment card data, patient insurance info, SSNs). Compromise exposes 50K+ patients to identity theft and financial fraud. The January ransomware incident and June crypto-miner both targeted this system, demonstrating actual attack interest. Loss of billing capability (4-day outage in Jan) directly impacts hospital revenue stream and cash flow; a week-long outage at a 350-bed facility = ~$2M in blocked revenue, cannot pay staff, suppliers, or operations.
