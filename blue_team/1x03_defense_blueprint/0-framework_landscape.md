# 0 The Framework Landscape

### Part 1 - Three-Framework Summary

* **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0:** Published by the [National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)](https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework), this framework helps organizations manage and reduce cybersecurity risks. It is structured around six core functions: **Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover**. It is widely used by organizations of all sizes and sectors as a high-level strategic roadmap to align cybersecurity risks with business goals.
* **CIS Controls v8:** Developed by the [Center for Internet Security (CIS)](https://www.cisecurity.org/controls), this framework provides a prioritized, actionable set of technical safeguards to stop common cyber attacks. It is organized into **18 top-level controls and specific safeguards** grouped into three Implementation Groups (IG1 to IG3). It is used by IT and security practitioners to achieve essential cyber hygiene and secure technical assets.
* **ISO/IEC 27001:** Published jointly by the [International Organization for Standardization (ISO)](https://www.iso.org/standard/27001) and the IEC, this standard defines requirements for establishing, implementing, and maintaining an Information Security Management System (ISMS). It is structured around mandatory clauses (4 through 10) and a comprehensive statement of applicability controls (Annex A). It is used globally by enterprises aiming to achieve formal, auditable third-party certification.

### Part 2 - Relationship Map

These three frameworks complement each other rather than compete, forming an end-to-end security program when used together. A clear mental model for their integration is that the [NIST CSF](https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework) answers **"What should we do?"** by defining high-level outcomes and governance; the [CIS Controls](https://www.cisecurity.org/controls) answer **"How should we do it?"** by providing granular technical safeguards; and [ISO 27001](https://www.iso.org/standard/27001) answers **"Can we prove we are doing it?"** through a rigorous, auditable management system.

### Part 3 - MedDefense Framework Selection

MedDefense should adopt the **NIST CSF 2.0** as its overarching strategic backbone, paired directly with **CIS Controls Implementation Group 1 (IG1)** for technical execution. As a regional hospital with a tiny team (one analyst and one deputy CISO) and a strict $120,000 budget, MedDefense cannot afford complex enterprise bureaucracy. NIST CSF provides the structural governance expected by regulators, while CIS IG1 delivers immediate, high-impact technical defenses (such as asset and patch management) to stop ransomware. This combination allows MedDefense to secure its systems efficiently and prove due diligence to the Board without needing expensive initial ISO 27001 certification audits.
